2. The Unified Triple-Core Design & QA Rules

Every interface change, file update, and layout proposal is mathematically verified against three core pillars of usability and high aesthetics:

Pillar 1: Apple Human Interface Guidelines (HIG)

Layouts must prioritize tactile responsiveness, native status configurations, and spring animations. We use a premium off-white canvas (#F8FAFC) and solid white container panels (#FFFFFF) bordered by sleek 1px borders to build elegant visual focus.

Pillar 2: Google Material Design 3 (M3)

Component bounds are rigidly contained. Interaction states are clear: buttons transform on click, cards scale subtly on hover, and focused inputs show clear active brand outlines. Tab switchers emulate physical slide elements.

Pillar 3: WCAG 2.2 AAA Accessibility Standards

Critical text legibility requires a minimum contrast of 7:1. Under MockMaxxing Light Mode:

  • Primary Text On White Cards: Dark Slate Black (#0C0C0C) on white provides 15+ contrast.
  • Dark Slate Blue On White Cards: Dark Slate Blue (#2D3142) on white provides 12.77:1 contrast.
  • Opaque Containment Rule: Coral text is strictly forbidden on white backgrounds due to failing checks. Vibrant Coral (#EF8354) is utilized exclusively as a background chip with **solid black overlay text** (#000000), ensuring a compliant **8.0:1 contrast ratio**.
  • Bleed Protection Mandate: Pinned/sticky elements (like header bars, sub-tab segments) must use 100% opaque backgrounds (e.g. solid #FFFFFF or solid #2D3142) to block scrolled items from overlapping and ruining contrast.
