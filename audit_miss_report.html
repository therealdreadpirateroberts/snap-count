EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: This post-audit report addresses the visual contrast bleed identified in the floating bottom navigation bar (AppTabBar). The translucency (glassmorphism) permitted content from active scrollviews (consensus sheets, draft feeds) to pass behind navigation labels. This document explains how this was overlooked, analyzes the technical root cause, and details the structural fix.

Why It Was Overlooked

During the recent comprehensive styling and performance review, the translucent background (Colors.glassSurface) was approved because it satisfied Apple HIG (Core Pillar 1) guidelines regarding native blurring and visual depth mapping. However, three main factors contributed to missing the scroll conflict:

Review Bias on Social Authentication: Critical focus was allocated to high-risk areas including onboarding Apple/Google OAuth contrast ratios, multi-tenant Zustand database isolation, and Monte Carlo background standings.
Static View Inspection: Visual inspections were performed primarily in a static state. The contrast degradation only manifests during active momentum scrolling when dynamic text directly overlaps the nav labels.
HIG Aesthetic Bias: The premium native blur was visually stunning, which biased the design review to prioritize "glassmorphism" aesthetics without vetting compliance with strict opaque overlays.
Framework Tension Analysis

The oversight occurred due to a direct structural tension inside our Unified Triple-Core Design & QA Framework. Specifically:

Core Pillar 1 (Apple HIG): Recommends lightweight translucent panels with thin borders and system blur to denote layers of containment.
Core Pillar 3 (WCAG 2.2 AAA Accessibility): Demands a minimum 7:1 contrast ratio for critical text elements and strictly mandates that floating overlays housing text must be 100% solid and opaque over high-density panels.

The system selected Apple's blur guidelines, failing to recognize that under movement, dynamic list rows passing behind the translucent bar would instantly break the WCAG 7:1 mathematical legibility ratio.

The Rectification and Systemic Safeguards

We have fully resolved the issue by immediately transitioning the AppTabBar stylesheet to a 100% solid, opaque obsidian background. This completely occludes background scrolling text while perfectly preserving the floating layout, thin boundaries, and tactile spring haptics.

Systemic Preventative Actions Implemented:

Accessibility Priority Override: Under our Triple-Core Framework rules, accessibility mandates (Core Pillar 3) now explicitly override aesthetic recommendations (Core Pillar 1) whenever they conflict.
Dynamic Motion Audits: All future design audits must be conducted with mock data under active scrolling momentum to ensure overlays perform flawlessly during view transitions.
